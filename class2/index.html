Flavors of Agile

Scrum:

  • Product Owner, Scrum Master, Devs
  • Two-week "sprints"
  • Focused empirical approach - evidence + learning
  • Release every sprint!

Kanban:

  • No defined team roles
  • Limited work-in-progress - can't do everything at once!
  • Continuous backlog grooming
  • Release when you're ready!

Above all: try, pick, and improve!

How to do Kanban for your MVP

  • Trello cards == user stories, need to be right-sized
  • Helpful to estimate your user stories!
  • Make cards actionable, hurts you to be vague
  • Use labels to mark Epics
  • Mentor acts like a scrum master (standups)
  • Limit your In Progress column!

Let's talk MVP more

Prioritize aggressively! Think:

  • What's most useful to the user?
  • How hard will each feature be to implement?
  • Does this feature depend on others? Vice versa?

Think of MVP like your user stories: right-sized

Why Design is Important

  • UX = User Experience
  • Users will make or break your software!
  • Good user design helps you sell products
  • Bad user design will scare users off
  • And: smart user design will influence user behavior

Wireframes: why, and how, and MVP

  • There's a few different tools in the kit
  • Goal is to establish everything early on!
  • Wireframes are cheaper than full prototypes
  • Design your MVP in wireframes, break down into stories

Code to your wireframes!

Behavior-Driven Development to follow your wireframe

  • GIVEN
    the car has been started
    and I am in first gear
  • WHEN
    I press the acceleration pedal
  • THEN
    the car will move forward

Tells you what code needs to happen before a user story is done!
